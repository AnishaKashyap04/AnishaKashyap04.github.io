Welcome

I am Dr. Anisha Kashyap, a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Inter-University Centre for Astronomy and Astrophysics (IUCAA). My research focuses on nonlinear dynamics, complex systems, and their application in astrophysics and biology. I specialize in recurrence quantification analysis (RQA), nonlinear time series, and network-based methods to study dynamical behavior in diverse systems.

I completed my Ph.D. in Physics from the University of Mumbai, where I modeled the swaying motion of branched structures like trees using analytical, numerical, and experimental approaches. My doctoral work involved Lagrangian modeling, chaos and synchronization studies, and time series analysis of real wind tunnel data.

With experience spanning theoretical modeling, numerical simulation, and data analysis, I explore complexity across natural and cosmic systems.

"From the rhythm of a swaying tree to the heartbeat of a distant binary star, I explore complex patterns across nature and the cosmos."
